# ADR-035 Phase 7a — 1 B Reference Validation

**Date:** 2026-04-27  
**ADR:** [ADR-035 Phase 7a Metrics Infrastructure](../adrs/mercury/ADR-035-phase7a-metrics-infrastructure.md)  
**Mercury version:** 1.7.45  
**JSONL artifact:** [`adr-035-phase7a-1b-2026-04-27.jsonl`](adr-035-phase7a-1b-2026-04-27.jsonl) — 22,256 records, 7.0 MB

## Purpose

Phase 5 of ADR-035's implementation plan: *"Status Accepted → Completed when all four phases land and a 1 B Reference end-to-end run produces the full JSONL artifact set."* This document records that run.

The artifact is the proof that every Phase 7a metric channel — bulk-load progress, rebuild progress with sub-phase identification, atom-store discrete events (rehash, file growth) and state samplers (intern rate, load factor, probe distance), process-level state (GC, LOH, RSS, disk-free) — emits cleanly at production scale.

## Run parameters

```
mercury \
  --store ~/Library/SkyOmega/stores/wiki-1b-ref-adr035 \
  --profile Reference \
  --bulk-load latest-all.ttl.bz2 \
  --rebuild-indexes \
  --limit 1000000000 \
  --metrics-out adr-035-phase7a-1b-2026-04-27.jsonl \
  --metrics-state-interval 60 \
  --no-repl
```

Two CLI invocations chained: bulk-load first, then `--rebuild-indexes` against the same store. State emission interval 60s — keeps JSONL volume manageable while capturing meaningful state evolution.

Source: `/Users/bemafred/Library/SkyOmega/datasets/wikidata/full/latest-all.ttl.bz2` (114 GB compressed Wikidata) — first 1 B triples streamed via the new ADR-036 BCL-only `BZip2DecompressorStream`. This is the first end-to-end production test that exercises both 7a (metrics) and 7b (bz2 streaming) together.

Hardware: Apple M5 Max, 128 GB RAM, NVMe SSD with 4.2 TB free.

## Highlights from the data

### `atom_rehash` event — ADR-028 working as designed

```jsonl
{"phase":"atom_rehash","ts":"...08:52:38...","old_buckets":268435456,"new_buckets":536870912,"duration_ms":12247.206}
```

A single rehash event 49 minutes into bulk-load, doubling 256 M → 536 M buckets in **12.25 seconds**. ADR-028's stop-the-world pause cost quantified live in production for the first time. The 75% load-factor trigger fired exactly once for the entire 1 B Reference load — second rehash never needed.

### `atom_probe_distance` evolution — hash distribution healthy

| Snapshot | Atoms | Buckets | Load factor | p50 | p95 | p99 | p999 | max |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Early (1 m in) | 4.2 M | 268 M | 1.5% | 0 | 0 | 0 | 1 | 3 |
| Pre-rehash (~26 m in) | 53 M | 268 M | ~20% | 0 | 2 | 5 | 8 | 16 |
| Late (~58 m in) | 213 M | 536 M | 39.7% | 0 | 2 | 4 | 7 | 16 |

p99 probe distance stays at 4 even at 39.7% load factor on 213 M atoms. **No regression from the 1.7.16 word-wise-FNV clustering incident.** The byte-wise FNV-1a's distribution holds up at production scale.

### `atom_file_growth` events — 16 GB final `.atoms`

```
1 GB → 2 GB at 4 m in
2 GB → 4 GB at 8 m in
4 GB → 8 GB at 16 m in
8 GB → 16 GB at 32 m in
```

Geometric doubling, four growths total. The 16 GB final size at 213 M atoms ≈ 75 bytes/atom — consistent with Wikidata's typical IRI lengths.

### Rebuild progress — sub-phase identification working

11,782 `rebuild_progress` records emitted across the 13:40 rebuild — average ~14 records/sec. Each carries `phase_name` (GPOS / Trigram), `sub_phase` (emission / drain), `entries_processed`, `estimated_total`, `rate_per_second`, plus `gc_heap_bytes` and `rss_bytes`. The silent-trigram-phase observability gap from Phase 6 (the limits entry that motivated 7a.1) is now closed.

### `load` records — `bz2` streaming transparent

10,000 records over 55:22 (one per 100 K triples per LoadProgress chunk-flush). Throughput stable at ~300 K triples/sec across the entire run; no perceptible slowdown attributable to bz2 streaming. The Phase 7b BCL-only decompressor's 33 MB/s steady-state has 4× headroom over the parser's ~8 MB/s consumption — the parser-side stages dominate, decompression never blocks. End-to-end behavior matches our pre-run analysis.

### Process-level state samplers

- **GC**: 67 events emitted across 69 min (~1/min). GC heap stayed at ~1.14 GB throughout — zero-GC discipline holding even at 1 B atoms.
- **LOH**: 68 deltas. No surprise spikes.
- **RSS**: 68 samples. Working set rose from ~700 MB at start to a peak of ~30 GB by end — entirely mmap-driven page cache, not managed-heap pressure.
- **Disk free**: 4266 GB → 4176 GB. ~90 GB consumed by the 1 B Reference store (matches the on-disk measurement).

## What this unblocks

- **Phase 7c — measured perf rounds**: every round has the metrics surface to produce before/after JSONL artifacts. The seven characterized rounds in `docs/limits/` (ADR-034 SortedAtomStore, bit-packed atom IDs, etc.) now have measurement infrastructure in place.
- **WDBench cold baseline**: the JSONL pattern + summary distribution shape demonstrated here transfers directly to query-side measurement. The harness in `benchmarks/Mercury.Benchmarks/WdBenchRunner.cs` produces the same record shape (`schema_version: "1"`, per-event records + summary).
- **Operator visibility on long Wikidata runs**: rebuild phases that took ~4 hours silently in Phase 6 now emit per-1M progress with sub-phase identification.
