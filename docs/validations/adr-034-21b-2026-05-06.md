## Headline measurements from instrumentation (commit `c79e590`)

### Sort dominates spill 12-16:1 across all scales

| Scale | Sample | Sort | Write | Ratio |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 10 M | chunk 0 (17.5 M records) | 5.26 s | 0.43 s | 12.2 : 1 |
| 100 M | mean of 18 chunks | 4.8 s | 0.30 s | 15.8 : 1 |
| 1 B | mean of 180 chunks | ~5.0 s | ~0.35 s | ~14 : 1 |
| 21.3 B | sample of late chunks | 4.5 s | 0.35 s | ~13 : 1 |

**Implication:** optimizing spill *write* throughput offers <10% wins; reducing sort cost or hiding it via pipelined-spill offers 12-16× larger wins. See `docs/limits/spill-blocks-parser.md` (Triggered, Round 2 candidate).

### Three-regime merge behavior at 21.3 B (new pattern, not visible at 1B)

| Regime | Scope | Rate | Cause |
|---|---|---|---|
| Warmup | first ~5% of records | ~0.3 M/s | page cache filling cold |
| Steady-state | middle ~80% | **~1.0-1.5 M/s** | cache-pressure-bound (4 TB intermediate ≫ 120 GB RAM) |
| Long-tail-cold-cache | final ~10% | ~0.2-0.5 M/s | rare atoms across small subset of cold-page chunks |

For comparison: 1 B run merged at **~6 M/s sustained** because 184 GB intermediate fit in 128 GB RAM. The 4-6× slowdown crossing the cache-fit boundary is structural. Pattern captured as `urn:sky-omega:pattern:merge-three-regimes` in Mercury.

### 8K cap fix validated at 21.3 B trigram drain

The cycle 8 first-attempt rebuild crashed at chunk-010131 in trigram drain — same FD-ceiling shape as cycles 1 and 4 (atom-merge). Cause: `ExternalSorter<T>.Merge` had its own k-way merge that opened chunks directly via `FileStream`, **bypassing** the BoundedFileStreamPool fix that was wired only into `SortedAtomStoreExternalBuilder.MergeAndWrite`.

Two-part fix:
- `dddfda3`: Wired `BoundedFileStreamPool` into `ExternalSorter<T>` — same architectural pattern as the atom-merge fix.
- `880bfe1`: Lowered `MergeFileStreamPoolHardCap` from 32K to 8K — the 32K theoretical cap is fictional vs macOS launchd's effective ~10,240 FD soft limit on spawned children.

**Trigram drain results with 8K cap:**
- 10,456 chunks total — would have crashed the 32K cap (matches cycle 8 first-attempt's chunk-010131 crash threshold)
- Pool sized to 8K → **2,456 chunks evict-and-reload (~23% miss rate)**
- 8,356 open FDs sustained throughout drain (well under OS limit)
- **No crash, no abort.** Drain completed in 8 h 24 m at 7.5 M/s sustained.

The eviction overhead added ~3-4 h to the drain phase. Round 2 candidate: hierarchical merge (cascade) to reduce active chunk count, or larger chunk size to reduce chunk count below the cap.

## Compared to Phase 6 baseline (v1 substrate, retired)

The Phase 6 21.3 B run completed in ~85 h on the retired v1 substrate (HashAtomStore, no prefix compression, no observability instrumentation, no pool/cap fixes). The cycle 8 r1 substrate represents:

- **2.0× wall-clock improvement** (46 h actual / 32 h clean vs 85 h baseline)
- **42% atom-store size reduction** (99 GB vs ~170 GB) via prefix compression + sorted layout
- **Sorted atom IDs** enabling integer range queries on atom prefixes (load-bearing for ADR-034 query-side wins)
- **Full instrumentation** (run_configuration, spill events, merge_progress, merge_completed, rebuild_progress, rebuild_phase, rebuild_complete) — Phase 6 had only one summary line per phase

## Round 2 optimization candidates (cycle 8 evidence-justified)

The cycle 8 measurements provide hard data for Round 2 prioritization:

| # | Optimization | Estimated win | Limits-register entry |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pipelined spill** (overlap sort with parser) | ~5 h saved on parser phase (eliminates 5.5 h sort-blocked time) | `spill-blocks-parser.md` |
| 2 | **Hierarchical merge (cascade)** for trigram drain | ~3-4 h saved (eliminates 8K cap eviction overhead) | new entry needed |
| 3 | **Intermediate cleanup hooks** at phase transitions | ~3.6 TB freed during run (vs end-of-run cleanup) | `intermediate-cleanup-deferred-to-run-end.md` |
| 4 | **Larger chunks for trigram drain** | reduces chunk count below 8K cap → 100% hit rate | composes with (2) |
| 5 | **Prefix-compress chunk format** (extend Round 2 to intermediate) | smaller intermediate fits cache better → eliminates three-regime tail | `external-merge-intermediate-disk-pressure.md` |
| 6 | **Runtime FD-limit detection** (getrlimit via P/Invoke) | replaces hard-coded 8K cap with detected limit on each platform | new entry needed |

Combined, these Round 2 mitigations could plausibly bring 21.3 B end-to-end to ~20-22 h (1.5× faster than r1's 32 h clean), or **4× faster than Phase 6 baseline**.

## Lessons captured for future rounds

Cycle 8 also surfaced behavioral discipline gaps that Mercury captured for future Sky Omega instances:

- **`urn:sky-omega:pattern:resource-limit-class-audit`** — every interaction with an OS-enforced resource is mandatory to mind; one occurrence is too many. Three FD-ceiling crashes (cycle 1, 4, 8) cost days because each was treated as a local fix.
- **`urn:sky-omega:pattern:merge-three-regimes`** — k-way merge has three distinct throughput regimes when intermediate exceeds RAM; the 1B trace cannot surface this.
- **`urn:sky-omega:pattern:third-path-dimension-shift`** — two-option framings share a hidden axis; the third path requires stepping off it (the original file-pool conversation that Martin forced).
- **`docs/limits/observability-coverage-gap.md`** — instrumentation discipline ("every >1-min operation emits progress") was the gating fix that turned cycle 8 from opaque-fail to characterized-success.
