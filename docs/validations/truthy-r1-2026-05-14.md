# Truthy r1 — 8.17 B Reference + Sorted on 1.7.57 (apples-to-apples companion to cycle 10 r4)

**Status (2026-05-14):** Truthy r1 production validation complete. Substrate at `wiki-truthy-ref-r1` loaded **8,171,214,990 triples** from the truthy-subset Wikidata dump and sealed in **14 h 13 m 28 s end-to-end** (vs cycle 10 r4 full's 23 h 56 m 50 s on the same 1.7.57 substrate, **−9 h 43 m / −40.6 %**). First measured truthy-subset run on the cycle 10 r4 / 1.7.57 substrate generation — the apples-to-apples external-comparison companion to the full measurement. MPHF construction characterized at 1.8 B atoms; trigram-rebuild characterized at production scale; substrate output sealed and queryable.

## Purpose

Most published Wikidata benchmark numbers (QLever, Virtuoso, Blazegraph WDQS comparisons, the WDBench paper's headline figures) run against `latest-truthy.nt.bz2` — the truthy subset of Wikidata. Cycle 10 Phase 3 r4's full measurement was against `latest-all.ttl.bz2` — a strictly larger workload that incumbent triplestore infrastructure must serve in production. **Both measurements stand independently as substrate-capability claims; only when paired do they support an apples-to-apples external comparison.** See [docs/memos/2026-04-30-latent-assumptions-from-qlever-comparison.md](../memos/2026-04-30-latent-assumptions-from-qlever-comparison.md) for the comparison-plane framing.

This truthy r1 measurement is the foundation artifact for that external comparison.

## ⚠ Dump-date confounder

| dataset | dump date | size compressed |
|---|---|---:|
| `latest-all.ttl.bz2` (full, cycle 10 r4) | **2026-04-03** | 114 GB |
| `latest-truthy.nt.bz2` (truthy r1) | **2026-05-08** | 40 GB |

**The two dumps are ~5 weeks apart.** Wikidata grows continuously, and growth is concentrated in literal-heavy predicates (`rdfs:label`, `schema:description`, `skos:altLabel`) — exactly the predicates the trigram index covers. Trigram-phase comparisons between cycle 10 r4 full and truthy r1 are confounded by ~5 weeks of Wikidata growth. The non-trigram phases (parse, merge, MPHF, GPOS) are less affected because they scale with structural counts.

A clean isolation of the **literal-density-per-triple effect** vs the **dump-date growth effect** would require a full dump from the same 2026-05-08 date (not run; ~13 h additional compute on this hardware). The combined effect is the substrate-shape claim worth publishing; isolating the two contributors is a follow-up question, not a precondition for the apples-to-apples comparison vs published WDBench numbers.

## The key finding — literal-density per triple

**Trigram entry count ratio truthy/full = 90.7 % at triple-count ratio 38.3 %.** That implies **~2.4× more trigram entries per triple in truthy than in full**.

Why: trigram entries come from literals. Truthy strips the **statement reification** layer (qualifiers, references, sitelinks) which is mostly non-literal (timestamps, IRIs, citation URLs). What remains is concentrated in literal-heavy predicates that truthy preserves intact:

- `rdfs:label` — preserved, every language Wikipedia exists in
- `schema:description` — preserved, multilingual descriptions
- `skos:altLabel` — preserved, aliases

So truthy has nearly as many literals as full (in absolute count) but ~38 % as many triples. Literals per triple is therefore ~2.4× higher in truthy. Trigram emission cost scales with literal character volume, not triple count.

**Implication for substrate-prediction**: any future Wikidata Mercury work needs **literal-volume scaling** for the trigram phase — not triple-count scaling. The other phases scale cleanly with triple count or atom count.

**Confounder**: as noted in the dump-date disclosure above, ~5 weeks of Wikidata growth between the dumps contributes some of the 90.7 % uplift (newer entities, more language coverage). A reasonable estimate is that a same-dated truthy would have trigram entries at ~75–83 % of full's, so the literal-density effect is the dominant contributor, but not the only one.

## FD trajectory

Truthy r1 trigram drain held ~1,400 simultaneously-open chunk FileStreams (vs full cycle 10 r4's 8,192). Per the same `ExternalSorter`-bypasses-`BoundedFileStreamPool` class concern documented in the cycle 10 r4 validation. Substantially more headroom at truthy scale — no FD pressure approaching the launchd ~10K trust gap. Class fix remains an open item for cycle 11.
