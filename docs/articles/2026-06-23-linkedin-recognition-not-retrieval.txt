Your AI can reason, reflect, and learn — inside one conversation. What it can't do is remember the last one.

That flaw was obvious the day the first ChatGPT shipped, and RAG — the standard fix — only papers over it: it retrieves text that is similar to your question, with no sense of how facts relate, when they were learned, or whether they were later overturned. So we built memory that works the way human memory does — recognition, not retrieval: surface what's associated, follow the connections outward, place each fact in time. It's called Lucy, it runs on an RDF triple store, and asked to recall one debugging subsystem it didn't hand back paragraphs — it reconstructed the whole arc across five sessions, dated and linked. RAG retrieves similar text; Lucy reconstructs known structure.
