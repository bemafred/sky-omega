# Aepistemics

## Canonical Definition

Aepistemics is the **condition of proceeding from emergence directly to engineering without epistemic clarification**.

It is not merely the absence of epistemic work — it is the structural inability or unwillingness to distinguish what is known from what is assumed before committing to construction.

Aepistemics is a diagnosis, not a label.
It identifies systems, practices, and organizations where:

- Discoveries are treated as specifications
- Assumptions are never surfaced or tested
- Engineering begins before understanding is earned
- Confidence is borrowed from activity rather than from clarity

Aepistemics is not specific to software. It is a **generic failure mode** that occurs wherever systems are built, decisions are made, or knowledge is applied — in engineering, medicine, policy, organizational design, and beyond. Software makes it visible because the feedback loops are short enough to expose the consequences, but the pattern is universal.

Aepistemics is the premature commitment to building what has not yet been understood.

---

## Non-Goals

- Aepistemics is not an insult or a tribal identity.
- It does not imply incompetence or malice.
- It is not the opposite of engineering — it is engineering without epistemic foundation.
- It does not claim that all epistemic work must be formal or exhaustive.

---

## Notes

- Aepistemics names the gap that EEE exists to prevent.
- A system built aepistemically may function, but its correctness is accidental rather than justified.
- The term is analogous to atheism — but where atheism names the absence of belief, aepistemics diagnoses the absence of the *process* by which belief becomes knowledge.
- Recognizing aepistemics is itself an epistemic act: it surfaces the unknown known that "we skipped understanding."
- Theism is a domain-specific instance of aepistemics — emergence (wonder, mystery) proceeding directly to engineering (dogma, ritual, commandments) without epistemic clarification. Atheism, historically framed as a counter-position, is more precisely understood as the recognition of aepistemics within the domain of existence. This reframing generalizes: AI hype, organizational theater, and uncritical policy-making are structurally identical — aepistemics applied to different domains.
